# 🎰 Las Vegas, USA — Desert Dry & Pest-Free

## 📍 Overview
- **Country/Region:** Nevada, United States
- **Bug Risk Level:** 🟢 Very Low
- **Best Season to Visit:** October – April
- **Difficulty to Visit:** Easy (direct flights, wide range of budgets)

## 🦗 Insect Profile
- **Mosquitoes:** Extremely rare — Mojave Desert climate prevents breeding
- **Flies:** Minimal — dry conditions keep fly populations very low
- **Ants:** Occasional in food areas, but well-managed
- **Spiders/Ticks:** Virtually none in urban/hotel areas
- **Other Pests:** Occasional scorpions in desert outskirts (not a concern in the Strip area)

## ✅ Why It's Bug-Free
- **Mojave Desert climate** — Less than 100mm annual rainfall, extreme aridity
- **Air-conditioned everything** — The Strip and all major resorts are fully climate-controlled
- **Municipal pest control** — Las Vegas has aggressive pest management programs
- **No standing water** in urban areas — drainage is engineered for desert conditions

## 🏨 Practical Tips
1. **October–April** is the ideal window — comfortable temperatures (15–25°C) and no bugs
2. The Strip is completely sealed and air-conditioned — you won't encounter a single insect
3. Avoid hiking in Red Rock Canyon or Valley of Fire without bug spray (rare desert gnats at dawn/dusk)
4. Hotels on the Strip have rigorous nightly cleaning protocols
5. If you venture to the Mojave Desert outside the city, carry bug spray for the rare desert mosquito at sunrise/sunset
