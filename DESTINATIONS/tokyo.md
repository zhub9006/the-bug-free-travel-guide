# 🏯 Tokyo, Japan — Winter Wellness

## 📍 Overview
- **Country/Region:** Japan, Tokyo
- **Bug Risk Level:** 🟢 Very Low
- **Best Season to Visit:** March – May / October – November
- **Difficulty to Visit:** Moderate (cost, language barrier)

## 🦗 Insect Profile
- **Mosquitoes:** Low in summer; dormant in winter months
- **Flies:** Very minimal in urban areas
- **Ants:** Occasional in summer, especially in older buildings
- **Spiders/Ticks:** Very rare indoors; seasonally present in grass areas
- **Other Pests:** Cockroaches can be present in older establishments (rare in modern hotels)

## 🌤️ Seasonal Breakdown
| Season | Mosquito Risk | Fly Risk | Overall Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spring (Mar–May) | Very Low | Very Low | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **Best** — Cherry blossoms, mild, few bugs |
| Summer (Jun–Aug) | Moderate | Low | ⭐⭐⭐ Hot & humid = more mosquitoes |
| Autumn (Sep–Nov) | Low | Very Low | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **Great** — Cool, comfortable, minimal bugs |
| Winter (Dec–Feb) | Non-existent | Non-existent | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ **Excellent** — Cold = zero insects |

## ✅ Why It's Bug-Free (in certain seasons)
- **Japan's rigorous cleanliness standards** — Regular building maintenance and pest management
- **Winter climate** — December–February temperatures eliminate insect activity entirely
- **Modern infrastructure** — Newer hotels and buildings are well-sealed and air-conditioned
- **Cultural practices** — Shoes removed at entry, reducing indoor pest access

## 🏨 Practical Tips
1. **March–May** or **October–November** offer the best balance of pleasant weather and minimal bugs
2. Stay in modern hotels (Shinjuku, Shibuya, Ginza districts) — well-sealed and regularly pest-treated
3. Avoid rural areas in summer — countryside has more mosquitoes and ticks
4. Convenience store food culture means you can eat indoors everywhere — minimal outdoor exposure
5. Japan's train system is indoors and fully enclosed — no bug exposure during transit
