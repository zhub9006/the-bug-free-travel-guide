# 🌸 Singapore — Clean City, Clear Skies

## 📍 Overview
- **Country/Region:** Singapore (city-state)
- **Bug Risk Level:** 🟢 Very Low
- **Best Season to Visit:** February – April
- **Difficulty to Visit:** Moderate (expensive, tropical)

## 🦗 Insect Profile
- **Mosquitoes:** Rare in urban core — government's aggressive vector control program keeps populations minimal
- **Flies:** Very minimal in urban areas; slightly more in wet markets
- **Ants:** Occasional in food areas, but rapid municipal response
- **Spiders/Ticks:** Very rare indoors; occasional in garden areas
- **Other Pests:** Well-managed through integrated pest management

## 🌤️ Seasonal Breakdown
| Season | Mosquito Risk | Fly Risk | Overall Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spring (Mar–May) | Very Low | Very Low | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **Best** — Dry, less rain = fewer bugs |
| Summer (Jun–Sep) | Low | Low | ⭐⭐⭐ Warm & wet, manageable |
| Autumn (Sep–Nov) | Moderate | Moderate | ⭐⭐⭐ Northeast monsoon = some increase |
| Winter (Dec–Feb) | Low | Low | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Cool & relatively dry |

## ✅ Why It's Bug-Free
- **World-leading vector control** — Singapore's National Environment Agency (NEA) runs one of the most aggressive mosquito surveillance and elimination programs in the world
- **Strict cleanliness laws** — Littering fines and mandatory building maintenance keep pest habitats minimal
- **Urban design** — Extensive air conditioning in all public spaces, malls, and transport
- **Wetland management** — Active drainage and mosquito larvicide in all water bodies

## 🏨 Practical Tips
1. **February–April** offers the driest weather with the lowest insect activity
2. Stick to the city center (Marina Bay, Orchard Road) — these areas are especially well-maintained
3. Avoid wet markets if you're sensitive — but urban food courts are pristine
4. All MRT stations and malls are air-conditioned and screened — minimal bug exposure
5. Hotels in Singapore have rigorous pest control certifications — look for NEA-licensed properties
